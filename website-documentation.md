### Overview of Music is Medicine
Music is Medicine is a charitable initiative focused on raising funds for brain cancer research through benefit concerts and related events. It is organized by PEAK Financial Group, a Montreal-based independent financial services firm, and operates as a fundraising platform rather than a standalone registered charity. All proceeds from its events are directed to support research at The Neuro (Montreal Neurological Institute-Hospital), part of McGill University Health Centre. The initiative emphasizes the healing power of music, transforming it into a tool for advancing medical research, better treatments, and hope for brain cancer patients and their families.

### Founding and Robert Frances' Role
The initiative was created by Robert Frances, the President and CEO of PEAK Financial Group. Frances, a Montreal resident, was diagnosed with a glioblastoma (a aggressive form of brain cancer) around 2020. During his treatment and recovery, he found solace and healing in music, which inspired him to launch Music is Medicine to give back to the medical community that saved his life. As a brain cancer survivor, Frances serves as the lead organizer and visionary, turning his personal passion for music into a purposeful movement for research funding. He has described the initiative as evolving from a personal coping mechanism to a broader effort with lasting impact, stating, "Today, it has purpose."

### History and Purpose
Launched in the early 2020s following Frances' diagnosis, Music is Medicine began as a response to the challenges of brain cancer treatment and the need for more research. Its core purpose is to fund clinical trials and studies at The Neuro, aiming to uncover the causes of brain cancer, develop innovative therapies, and improve patient outcomes. The initiative operates under PEAK's #PEAKWithPurpose banner, which promotes community impact. It has grown from a single event into an annual benefit, fostering partnerships with sponsors and collaborators to amplify its reach.

Overall, the initiative has mobilized community support, corporate sponsorships, and awareness, with plans for continued growth.
